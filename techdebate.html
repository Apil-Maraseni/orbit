About

Orbit Tech Debate offers a dynamic platform for organized discussions on cutting-edge technology topics. Participants engage in insightful conversations, express viewpoints, and share ideas with peers and distinguished guests. Topics range from cybersecurity and AI to emerging technologies, providing a stimulating environment to explore diverse perspectives. This competition aims to foster critical thinking, research, and presentation skills, encouraging participants to substantiate their arguments with well-founded evidence.

Structure

1. Activity: Structured debate on technology-related topics.

2. Team Size: Solo participants.

3. Debate topics:

-The first theme will be announced in advanced.

-For subsequent rounds, participants will face surprise topics to test their ability to think quickly and respond in real-time.

4. Judging Criteria:

-Content Relevance (40%): Focus on accuracy and relevance to the topic.

-Clarity & Presentation (30%): Clear communication and coherent structure.

-Persuasiveness & Engagement (30%): Effective engagement with the topic and opponent's points.

5. Time Limits: 15 min.

FAQs

1. Who can participate?
The debate is open to solo participants from high schools, colleges, or individuals with an interest in technology.

2. What are the debate topics?
The first debate topic will be announced beforehand. Later rounds will feature surprise topics to test participants' impromptu skills.

3. Will internet access be provided? Yes, internet access will be provided to search up for facts for a prespecified amount of time. 4. How will the event be judged?
Debates will be judged on content relevance, clarity, and persuasiveness.

5.Is there a registration fee?
Yes, a Rs 250 registration fee is required for participation.

Rules

Team Size: Solo participants only.

Time Management: Participants must strictly adhere to the time limits for speeches, rebuttals, and closing statements. Failure to comply will result in point deductions.

Relevance: All statements and arguments must be directly related to the debate topic. Tangential or irrelevant discussions will not be tolerated.

Research: Participants are expected to conduct thorough research on the topics. Comprehensive preparation is crucial for presenting well-founded arguments.

Code of Conduct: Personal attacks, offensive language, or disrespectful behavior will lead to disqualification.

Decision: The judges' decisions are final.
